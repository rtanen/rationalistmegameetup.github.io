East Coast Rationalist Megameetup 2017

TL;DR: We put together a weekend-long gathering of rationalist community people in NYC for the 2017 Solstice weekend.

As you might already know, the seventh annual Secular Solstice celebration happened this year in New York City on December 9, 2017. This meant we had a whole bunch of people from the rationalist community all in town for the same weekend.

Since many of us needed a place to stay anyway, we rented a big house together in Astoria, Queens, and used that as the central gathering place, like at Highgarden in 2014. This way we got to spend the weekend hanging out together, with more flexibility to do things than if we all had to wander around looking for a public space.

Besides Solstice and the afterparty, the big activity was an unconference on Saturday afternoon. We also had a ritual lab, games, meals together, and other activities people wanted to run! There was also plenty of room for unstructured socializing, of course.

This all cost $120.26 per person, which covered the rental plus food and other expenses. For this, attendees got to sleep in the house Friday through Sunday nights (or whatever subset of those they could make it), have three meals with us (Saturday lunch and dinner and Sunday breakfast), and hang out with a bunch of nice/cool/awesome ingroup people throughout the weekend. (Solstice tickets were not part of this deal.)

Attendees who didn’t want to sleep in the house paid the reduced rate of $25.00 to help cover food and other expenses.

Registration was on a first-come-first-served basis, until we ran out of room.

If you want to read the original master plan, it’s here. Please be aware that some details are now out of date.

Special thanks to everyone who preregistered and helped make this event possible!
